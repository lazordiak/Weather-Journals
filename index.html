How is the weather where you are? Is it raining? Are the clouds running by or moving lazily? Is the sun high in the sky, or is it evening, or is it night? Wherever you are, the visuals you're looking at now are a representation of the weather in New York City at the moment you loaded the page, and you have stumbled into Weather Journals -- good to see you. : )


Weather Journals is an attempt to put people in touch with their surroundings and with each other. The whole thing is powered by OpenAI's GPT2 algorithm, which is a machine learning algorithm that generates human-like text.


Here's how it works:


First, we'll ask you to submit a reflection of the weather where you are in the box on the top left. This can be whatever you want: what do the clouds look like? How does the weather make you feel? Does the wind make you happy? Does the rain sound nice? Does it remind you of another time? What was that like?


After you've put in your reflection, you can adjust the length and the creativity of the story you'd like the weather to write you in return. Then hit the button, wait a bit and you'll get your story delivered to you by the weather.


At the end of the day, all the reflections that everyone put into Weather Journals for that day are collected, and the machine learning model is trained on them. What that means is that every day into the future, the model reflects all the reflections of all the days before it. It grows and evolves with the weather and with our perception of the weather.
